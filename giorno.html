Welcome
Built for bodies that don't follow the rules
Giorno is a symptom tracker designed specifically for people with complex, fluctuating health conditions — the kind that are hard to explain, often invisible, and rarely fit a standard medical form.

It takes about 60 seconds to log each day. After a week, it starts showing you patterns you might not have noticed.
Free to use — genuinely, not just as a trial
Managing a chronic illness is already hard enough without a paywall on the tools that help you understand your own body. Giorno's core features — daily logging, symptom tracking, insights, history, medications, and more — are free for everyone and will stay that way.

When Giorno Pro launches, it will only add optional extras: things that go beyond the essentials. Nice-to-haves like advanced analytics or integrations that you might enjoy, but nothing you need to get real value from the app. The fundamentals will never be locked.

Everyone deserves to understand what's happening in their body. That's the whole point.

What is the Autonomic Load Estimate?
Giorno includes a feature that estimates your autonomic nervous system state from your symptoms. Here's exactly what it's based on — and what it isn't.
Sleep quality and HRV Research consistently shows that poor sleep reduces heart rate variability the following day. Subjective sleep ratings correlate with objective HRV measurements at r≈0.6–0.7.
Tobaldini et al., 2017 — PubMed ↗
Fatigue severity and autonomic function In ME/CFS populations, self-reported fatigue severity correlates with reduced HRV and sympathetic dominance. The COMPASS-31 scale (which includes orthostatic and fatigue domains) is a validated clinical tool for autonomic dysfunction.
Suarez et al., 2012 — PubMed ↗
Post-exertional symptom patterns Studies in ME/CFS and POTS show that symptom severity two days after exertion predicts autonomic recovery better than same-day measures — supporting the boom-bust pattern detection in Giorno.
Davenport et al., 2019 — PubMed ↗
What it is not: The Autonomic Load Estimate is not a clinical measurement, not a diagnosis tool, and not a substitute for medical advice. It's a personal pattern-awareness tool based on published correlations. Giorno will always label it clearly as an estimate.

What is the Autonomic Load Estimate?
Giorno is not a medical device. The Autonomic Load Estimate is a personal pattern-awareness tool — not a clinical measurement, not a diagnostic instrument, and not a substitute for medical care. It should never be used to make medical decisions. It is described as an estimate throughout the app and always will be.
The estimate is built from your own logged symptoms using correlations identified in published research. Here's exactly what it's based on.
Sleep quality predicts next-day HRV
Multiple studies show that self-reported sleep quality correlates with objective heart rate variability measurements the following morning. Poor sleep reduces parasympathetic tone and increases sympathetic activity — both reflected in lower HRV. Giorno uses your logged sleep quality as the strongest single input to the estimate.
Tobaldini et al. (2017). Sleep, sleep deprivation and the autonomic nervous system. Best Practice & Research Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism. PubMed ↗
Fatigue severity correlates with autonomic dysfunction
In ME/CFS and related populations, self-reported fatigue severity is consistently associated with reduced HRV and autonomic dysregulation. The COMPASS-31 — a validated clinical scale for autonomic dysfunction — includes fatigue and orthostatic domains that mirror the symptoms Giorno tracks.
Suarez et al. (2012). The autonomic symptom profile. Neurology. PubMed ↗
Post-exertional patterns reveal autonomic recovery
Research in ME/CFS shows that symptom severity 24–48 hours after exertion is a more reliable marker of autonomic recovery than same-day measurements. This is the basis for Giorno's boom-bust pattern detection — identifying when a high-energy day is followed by a crash.
Davenport et al. (2019). Chronotropic intolerance. Frontiers in Pediatrics. PubMed ↗
Orthostatic symptoms as ANS markers
Dizziness on standing, heart racing, and heat intolerance are direct symptoms of autonomic dysregulation — particularly in POTS and dysautonomia. Their severity on a given day is meaningful signal for the overall autonomic load estimate.
Freeman et al. (2011). Consensus statement on the definition of orthostatic hypotension. Clinical Autonomic Research. PubMed ↗
Known limitations
Giorno is transparent about what its estimates can and can't do:

It becomes more accurate over time. The estimate is personalised to your patterns — it's less reliable in the first week and improves with consistent logging.

Inconsistent logging reduces accuracy. If you log sporadically, the patterns are harder to detect reliably.

It's calibrated for autonomic conditions. If your primary symptoms aren't fatigue, sleep, or orthostatic in nature, the estimate may be less relevant to you.

Individual variation matters. The underlying correlations are based on population research. Your personal relationship between these symptoms and your ANS state may differ.

About the creator
Hi, I'm Stephanie — a mental health therapist, and the person behind Giorno.

For as long as I can remember, I've felt like something in my body wasn't quite right. In 2022, while juggling graduate school for clinical counseling and a full-time job, things started to shift. My health noticeably declined — symptoms that once felt manageable became overwhelming, showing up more often and with greater intensity. I was constantly drained, like I could never fully recharge.

I'm still figuring out what's going on. I'm currently being evaluated for several conditions, including narcolepsy type 1, and came very close to meeting the diagnostic criteria across two sleep studies.

I built Giorno to help me pace myself and understand the patterns that affect me — because I've learned that knowing your patterns is part of living your life as fully as you can.

It's been helpful to me, so I wanted to share it with you. I hope it helps you manage your own conditions better, and that you're able to live your life to the fullest that you can.

Plain-English version What Giorno collects
If you use Giorno without an account: nothing. All data stays on your device in your browser's local storage.

If you create an account for cloud sync: your email address and your encrypted health logs (symptoms, medications, notes). Your password is hashed and never stored in plain text.

What Giorno never does
Sells your data. Uses it for advertising. Shares it with third parties. Uses it to train AI models (without explicit opt-in). Tracks you across websites. Installs analytics or advertising SDKs.

Data retention
Your data is retained as long as your account exists. You can delete your account and all associated data at any time from the Sync tab. Deletion is immediate and irreversible.

Security
Health data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest. We use industry-standard security practices.

Your rights
You have the right to access, export, correct, or delete all data associated with your account at any time. To exercise these rights, use the export and delete functions in the app, or contact us.
